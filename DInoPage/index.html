About Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the terrestrial ecosystems for over 160 million years.

They varied greatly in size, appearance, and behavior, ranging from small, feathered creatures to colossal, fearsome predators.

Featured Dinosaur: Iguanodon

The Iguanodon, a colossal herbivorous dinosaur, roamed the Earth during the Early Cretaceous period, approximately 125 million years ago.

Its name, meaning "iguana tooth," was inspired by its teeth's resemblance to those of the modern-day iguana.

Interesting Facts

  • Dinosaurs first appeared during the Triassic period around 230 million years ago.
  • The largest known dinosaur is the Argentinosaurus, estimated to have been around 100 feet long and weighed about 100 tons.
  • Some dinosaurs were warm-blooded, while others were cold-blooded.
